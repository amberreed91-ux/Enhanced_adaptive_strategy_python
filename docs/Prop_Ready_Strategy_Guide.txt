PART 1) THE BIG IDEA (IN PLAIN ENGLISH)
====================================================================
Your strategy is a quality filter, not a constant signal generator.
It only wants to trade when multiple conditions agree:

1) Regime quality
   - It wants TRENDING conditions (not random chop).

2) Trend efficiency
   - It requires meaningful directional movement (efficiency >= 0.55).

3) Structure direction
   - For LONG: bullish structure + bullish HTF bias.
   - For SHORT: bearish structure + bearish HTF bias.

4) Confluence score
   - Many components combine into one quality score.
   - If confluence is too low, no trade.

5) Risk-first sizing
   - Position size is based on stop distance and dollar risk.
   - This is crucial for longevity.

6) Daily limits
   - Max trades/day, max loss/day, max losing streak.
   - In Prop Mode, limits are tighter and can lock the day.

PART 3) PROP EVALUATION MODE (HOW TO THINK)
====================================================================
Prop mode is about survival + consistency, not hero returns.

When Prop Mode is ON, your script hardens:
- Lower risk cap per trade
- Higher confluence requirement
- Longer cooldown between trades
- Optional quality-only time windows
- Tighter daily limits and lockout behavior

Mindset shift:
- A+ setups only
- Lower frequency
- Protect drawdown at all costs

Daily rule card:
[ ] I will stop when daily lockout triggers.
[ ] I will not increase size to recover losses.
[ ] I will not override filters because of FOMO.


====================================================================
PART 4) MGC VS MNQ (WHY THEY FEEL DIFFERENT)
====================================================================
MNQ (Micro Nasdaq):
- Faster movement, more whipsaw risk, higher noise.
- Needs stricter short gate and cleaner alignment.

MGC (Micro Gold):
- Different rhythm and session behavior.
- Can trend well but reacts differently around macro events.

Key point:
Do NOT assume one instrument's behavior transfers perfectly.
Your script handles this with instrument-aware gates and sizing.

PART 7) COMMON NEW-TRADER MISTAKES (AND FIXES)
====================================================================
Mistake: Taking every signal.
Fix: Only A-grade context + confluence.

Mistake: Increasing size after losses.
Fix: Keep size algorithmic and capped.

Mistake: Ignoring daily stop.
Fix: Hard stop trading when limits are hit.

Mistake: Judging strategy by 1-3 trades.
Fix: Evaluate over meaningful sample size.

Mistake: Changing settings daily.
Fix: Make changes only after structured review.
